Fresher Survival Kit

College is an exciting new chapter filled with opportunities, challenges, friendships, and personal growth. This survival kit contains practical advice from seniors to help you avoid common mistakes, manage academics effectively, maintain a healthy lifestyle, and make the most of your first year at Amrita Amaravati.

✅ Do's Every Fresher Should Follow

Attend Classes Regularly

Attendance and internals matter. Missing classes can make exams much harder.

Talk to Seniors

Seniors can guide you about courses, faculty, clubs and internships.

Join Clubs

Participate in technical and cultural clubs to improve skills and networking.

Learn Beyond Classrooms

Use platforms like LeetCode, GeeksforGeeks and NPTEL from your first year.

Build Friendships

Your friends will become your support system during assignments and exams.

Maintain Notes

Good notes make exam preparation much easier.

❌ Don'ts Every Fresher Should Avoid

Don't Ignore Internals

Many students focus only on end-semester exams and regret it later.

Don't Procrastinate

Assignments pile up quickly if you keep postponing them.

Don't Skip Labs

Labs contribute to your practical knowledge and internal marks.

Don't Depend Only on Faculty

Self-learning is an important part of engineering education.

Don't Compare Yourself Constantly

Everyone learns at a different pace. Focus on improvement.

Don't Stay Isolated

Participate in activities and interact with classmates.

⚠️ Common Mistakes Seniors Made

  • Waiting until exams to start studying.
  • Ignoring coding during first year.
  • Thinking CGPA can be fixed in the final semester.
  • Missing club opportunities because of hesitation.
  • Not exploring internships and competitions early.
  • Spending too much time on social media.
  • Not asking doubts when they were confused.
  • Ignoring health during busy semesters.
💡 Most seniors agree that consistency beats last-minute hard work.

⏰ Managing Studies & Activities

College is not just academics. The key is balancing studies, clubs, sports and personal time.

Weekdays

Focus on classes, assignments and coding practice.

Evenings

Use club activities and sports for skill development and relaxation.

Weekends

Revise subjects, complete pending work and learn new technologies.

Daily Habit

Even 30–60 minutes of coding every day creates huge improvement over time.

🧠 Mental Health & Stress Management

Engineering can sometimes feel overwhelming, especially during exam periods. Taking care of yourself is just as important as academics.

Get Enough Sleep

Sleep improves concentration and memory retention.

Stay Physically Active

Sports, walking and exercise help reduce stress.

Talk to Someone

Share concerns with friends, seniors, faculty or family members.

Take Breaks

Continuous studying without breaks reduces productivity.

Don't Fear Failure

One bad quiz or one bad semester doesn't define your future.

Celebrate Small Wins

Completing assignments, improving grades and learning new skills all matter.

🌟 Final Advice: Your first year is about adapting, learning and exploring. Focus on progress, not perfection.
